## One line

A Graveyard-Keeper-styled dark-fantasy RPG set on a village's dying edge — where the ground has begun, quietly, to *listen*.

## Short description

**Raven Hollow** is a medieval pixel action-RPG in the dark-fantasy world of **Draconia**. You arrive at a human border village during the Long Vigil, take up one of six class kits, and work five quests for the people who live here — an innkeeper whose well has turned copper, a smith with a dagger that won't stay clean, a miller's daughter found standing at the treeline with her head tilted, listening to something no one else can hear. None of them end cleanly. The dread is ambient. The ground is patient.

## Long description

Raven Hollow wears its inspirations openly — the readable interface of *World of Warcraft*, the loot grammar of *Diablo II*, the lived-in village craft loop of *Graveyard Keeper* — and pours them into a hand-built corner of the author's own dark-fantasy universe, **Draconia**.

The demo opens in the village itself: a bustling square, an inn, a smithy, a cemetery, nineteen townsfolk who each look and speak like a person rather than a spawn. Choose one of six classes, each with a three-button ability kit and animated spell effects, and you're loose in a world that keeps time — a day/night cycle warms the lanterns at dusk and doubles the wolves' hunger after dark.

East of town, past a stone gatehouse, the **Emberfall Road** runs into a border forest thick enough to get lost in: an abandoned hunter's camp, standing stones carved with angular runes no one should read aloud, a boar wallow, a wolf den watched over by an old bear, and an orc war-camp that has crept too close to the road. It is alive — deer, foxes, and rabbits scatter as you pass; wolves and boars do not.

Underneath the questing and the loot is Draconia's quiet horror: a symptom ladder the villagers won't name. The well goes copper. The yeast dies. The dust aligns. People begin to *listen*. The demo's five quests each brush against it, and not one of them offers a clean win.

## Key features

- **Six classes**, each with a distinct three-ability kit (mouse-aimed) and animated spell VFX — Warrior, Rogue, Mage, Paladin, Necromancer, and the Hunter.
- **Loot that matters** — Diablo-style rarities, five hand-seeded legendaries, a WoW-style backpack and a drag-and-drop paper-doll character sheet with visible equipped gear.
- **Five lore quests** drawn from Draconia canon, with branching moral choices — bury the cursed dagger or keep it; strike the orc camp or slip away and warn the town. No clean endings.
- **A living world** — a full day/night cycle, a bustling town of unique villagers, and a wilderness of huntable and dangerous fauna.
- **Crafting** — cull wolves, boars, and the dead for pelts, hides, bone, and ember-dust; work them at Goran's forge and the inn hearth into gear and draughts.
- **Readable, ornate UI** — gold-bezel HUD, live unit frames with click-to-target, a framed minimap, and a parchment world map.
- **Save/continue**, main menu, and a demo level cap of 10 on a deliberately slow curve.

## The world (for interviewers)

Draconia is a 115-page dark-fantasy canon; Raven Hollow is one human border village within it during the **Long Vigil**. The demo's throughline is the **Underlanguage** — a creeping affliction the villagers experience as a ladder of symptoms (the ground warms, wells run copper, yeast dies, dust aligns, and finally people begin to *listen*) rather than a monster to fight. It is meant to be felt, not explained; the demo ends on it, unresolved, as the Kickstarter cliffhanger.
