# CALENDAR EVENTS — The Draconian Year
### WoW seasonal calendar, Draconia lore · Raven Hollow design doc

**Canon sources:** `_lore_extract.txt` (Parts II cosmology, IV regions, V factions, VII/VIII cast, XI hooks) · `WORLD_PLAN.md` (40 zones) · `scripts/quest_defs.gd` (quest schema) · `scripts/npc_data.gd` (existing cast — Innkeeper Marta's **Ember Hearth**, Farmer Ansel's "harvest fair," Gatewarden Iosif's **Emberfall Road** are already canon in-repo and are reused below).

**Design law for every festival (the Draconia rule):** the cheerful thing is real — people genuinely feast, brew, court, and light fires — **and** every festival exists *because* of the horror underneath it. Folk custom in Draconia is fossilized survival protocol: masks, vigils, empty bowls, and counted bells all started as countermeasures against the Bloodstone, the Veil, or the Underlanguage, and most celebrants have forgotten why. The player has not. One quest per festival quietly shows the machinery. The Bloodstone **arranges** — so each festival is also a date the stone knows is coming.

**Systems notes (grounded in current engine):**
- Festivals are quest *packs* gated by real-world date windows (server clock), delivered through the existing `QuestDefs` schema — `giver`, `prereq`, `objectives` (talk/kill/reach/choice/use_item), `turn_in_npc`, `aftermath`, `auto_trigger`. A festival adds a `season` key (new): `{window:"06-21..07-05"}`; `Quests` skips offering/auto-triggering out-of-window quests. Incomplete festival quests are abandoned (not failed) at window close — WoW rules.
- **XP stays grind-honest:** festival quests pay the *low* end of the quest-XP band for their level (they're repeatable yearly; the draw is cosmetics, consumables, titles, and lore — WoW-Classic holiday economy, not catch-up mechanics).
- **Rewards vocabulary:** each festival has a token currency + a themed reward table (dress items via the equipment slots in `items.gd`, stat-light trinkets, buff food/flasks, one vanity legendary per festival earnable only across multiple years — the MMO retention hook).
- **The Bloodstone teases through every festival** (WotLK Lich-King cadence): each festival contains exactly one moment — an inscription stone, a dream, a wrong note in a song — where the machine speaks *to the player personally*. These are marked **[BLOODSTONE BEAT]** and chain, in order across the year, into the meta-thread "The Arranged Year" (finale feeds the Grave & Bloodstone Pit descent).

## 1. THE KEPT NAMES — *Lunar Festival* (Jan 23 – Feb 10)

**Lore meaning.** WoW honors the elders; Draconia's elders are *walking*. Once a year, in the coldest stretch, every settlement reads its dead aloud — every name, in order, all the way back — because folk belief says a name spoken by the living is a name the North cannot claim. The custom is older than anyone knows and more correct than anyone suspects: a name kept warm in a living mouth is *specific, imperfect memory* — exactly the substance the Bloodstone cannot index. The Kept Names is the continent unknowingly feeding the Pause once a year. Lanterns are lit at graves; coins are pressed into headstones ("a coin for the ferryman he never needed"). In Black Night, the Council of Six holds its own grotesque mirror ceremony: Moasa reads the containment rolls aloud. Nobody claps.

**Hub zone.** Raven Hollow graveyard (Gravekeeper Vasile is the festival vendor/anchor — his existing "everyone ends up on my hill" dialogue is the festival's thesis), with elder-lantern pilgrimage targets in all four capitals (the WoW "honor the Elders" travel loop — one lantern per major zone, discovery XP, teaches the map).

**Event quests (giver → one-liner):**
1. **"Every Name, In Order"** (Vasile) — hold the torch while Vasile reads the hill; when he falters on one name, find the sunken headstone he's avoiding and learn why. *(talk + reach)*
2. **"A Coin for Petra"** (Old Petra) — carry remembrance-coins to three named graves along the old road for the friends whose knees gave out before hers. *(reach ×3)*
3. **"The Lantern Line"** (festival board) — light the elder-lanterns of all four capitals; each faction's keeper tells you, differently, the same lie about why. *(reach ×4, capitals — the travel-loop quest)*
4. **"The Roll of the Pit"** (Pit-Caller Drego, Sangeroasa) — stand beside Drego over the forge-roar and confirm, from the nailed ledger, that his roll-call of the Debt Pit's dead has never once been wrong. *(use_item: ledger rubbing)*
5. **"Names Under Regulation"** (Threadwarden Ilka, Black Night) — Ilka names the shells she tends, which regulations forbid; carry her whispered list somewhere the Council can't file it. *(talk → reach; choice: deliver to Vasile's hill or burn it safe)*
6. **"The Unread Letter"** (Bent Oar board) — the Courier who died in the Chamber never delivered his sealed letter; find who is still waiting for an answer and decide whether they get it. *(reach → choice — the seal stays UNbroken either way; reading is the vector)*
7. **"Twelve, Facing Out"** (Vera Cold-Hands, Blestem) — buy the names of an executed row of twelve and chalk them in the dead-end alley where the boot-prints face the wall — facing *out*. *(use_item ×12; Strigoi enforcers take notice — stealth flavor)*
8. **"What the Living Owe"** (Quartermaster Helva, Angel Wings) — audit Helva's private ledger of orphans fed against the roll of parents lost; deliver the awful, warm arithmetic to Maren. *(talk chain)*
9. **[BLOODSTONE BEAT] "One Name Too Many"** (auto-trigger, graveyard, night) — the year's roll is done, and one lantern is still burning over a grave nobody lit; the headstone bears the player-character's name, undated. An inscription stone nearby reads, in shifting orange: *"I keep names too."*

**Rewards theme.** Lantern off-hands, grave-cedar incense (night-resist buff), the **Coppervein**-style remembrance rings, festival dumplings (Marta's), title **"the Remembered."** Multi-year vanity: **Vasile's Second Shovel** (Gren's shovel — cruelty and care from the same hands).

**The dark twist.** The reading works — that's the horror. Zones whose rolls were read *completely* this year measurably calm (fewer thread-touched dead spawn for a month). Zones where the player skipped or shortened the roll get *worse*. Then the hook lands: Moasa's containment roll in Black Night is the longest in the world, read by one clerk, alone — and this year, for the first time in six centuries, she asks the player to help, because *the roll has been getting longer than the graves can account for*. Someone — something — is adding names in advance.

## 2. THE COURTING OF SECRETS — *Love is in the Air* (Feb 3 – 16)

**Lore meaning.** In Blestem, where information is the hard currency, courtship was never flowers — it is the ritual exchange of *leverage*. Once a year, under the festival the Strigoi drily call the Courting, the maze-city plays its great game in the open: sealed letters change hands, each containing one true secret about the sender. To offer a secret is to offer a knife to your own throat, handle first; to accept is to hand yours back. It is the most honest the Strigoi ever get, which is why they only risk it once a year and dress it as a party. The custom has spread continent-wide in cheaper forms — humans exchange promise-letters, Varcolaci exchange debts of honor, and the Iele exchange nothing, standing a little closer together than usual, in rows. **No romance mechanics — the currency is trust: letters, leverage, loyalty.** (GoT register: every valentine is a hostage negotiation.)

**Hub zone.** Blestem, the Lower Market (Vera Cold-Hands runs the festival's letter-exchange stall: *"pays extra for kindnesses, not secrets"* — her canon detail is the festival's soul). Satellite letter-posts at the Bent Oar and Angel Wings.

**Event quests:**
1. **"Handle First"** (Vera Cold-Hands) — write your own festival letter: choose which true thing about yourself to seal (three options drawn from your actual quest history — the `aftermath` system reads your record); someone in Blestem will buy it. *(choice — the chosen secret has consequences at next year's Courting: multi-year hook)*
2. **"The Unsigned One"** (festival board) — a letter with no sender is moving through the Market and everyone who reads it goes quiet; intercept it before it reaches the Black Spire. *(reach chain; the letter is blank — the readers are lying about what they saw)*
3. **"A Kindness, Priced"** (Vera) — Vera will pay triple for a *warm* story with a name attached; find one in Blestem — harder than it sounds — and decide if selling it cheapens it. *(talk ×3 → choice)*
4. **"Twelve Proposals"** (Nistor the Sightline Sweeper) — deliver twelve sealed Courting letters along Nistor's sweep-route without once being observed reading the addresses; he knows every blind spot and sells you the map, one corner short. *(reach ×12, stealth-lite)*
5. **"The Queen's Regrets"** (Fielderine's spymaster, Angel Wings) — Fielderine receives a Courting letter from Cazimir himself; carry her answer east and do not lose it, because both letters together would end the cold war — badly. *(escort-style reach, ambushes)*
6. **"The Forged King's Hand"** (Hammer-Widow Olga, Sangeroasa) — Valrom's court demands a festival letter from every forge-family as loyalty proof; forge Olga's for her, because what her sons would write is true. *(use_item: forged letter; choice — a whiff of the ledger-forging from lore QS-1)*
7. **"Letters to the Still"** (Threadwarden Ilka) — pilgrims leave Courting letters in the hands of entranced kin standing in the Listening Steppe; collect last year's — unread, weathered, still clutched — and return them to the senders who are ready to grieve. *(reach ×5 — the festival's heavy one)*
8. **"The Lift-Boy's Valentine"** (the Unnamed Lift-Boy, Riddler's Quarter) — the boy who owns nothing but the lift's true pattern wants one letter delivered by a route only he knows; ride the pattern, ask nothing, deliver it. *(reach, disorientation-engine gimmick — cheerful, genuinely sweet)*
9. **"What Cazimir Collects"** (Sabira, veiled) — Sabira hires you to retrieve *her* letter from the exchange before Cazimir reads it; her managed blankness cracks exactly once, and only sideways. *(reach + stealth; tell: "Poor bastards.")*
10. **[BLOODSTONE BEAT] "Return to Sender"** (auto-trigger, any letter-post, last day) — among your festival mail is a letter you never wrote, in your handwriting, sealed with warm wax that smells of copper: *"You will give me one true thing about yourself. Everyone does, in the end. — B."*

**Rewards theme.** Signet rings, sealed-letter trinkets (single-use "call in a favor" consumables — a faction guard looks away once), leverage tokens redeemable for Blestem reputation, grey-rose lapel cosmetics, title **"the Confidant."** Multi-year vanity: **Cazimir's Empty Envelope** (nobody knows what it held; that's the power of it).

**The dark twist.** The festival *is* Cazimir's harvest. The Courting was instituted by the Black Spire six centuries ago: one fortnight a year when the whole continent voluntarily writes down its truest weaknesses and hands them to couriers. The archive of every Courting letter ever exchanged is a floor of the Spire — and at the festival's end, the player is shown one drawer of it, and their own Quest-1 letter already filed inside, annotated in two hands. One is Cazimir's. The other ink is violet, and no one at the Spire will say whose it is.

## 3. SOWING DAY — *Noblegarden* (spring week, ~Apr 26 – May 2)

**Lore meaning.** The spring sowing festival of the human west: painted seed-stones (clay eggs packed with seed-grain and a wish written on the shell) are hidden in the furrows by night and hunted by children at dawn — whoever finds one plants it, and the field it grows in is "spoken for" against a bad year. It is Draconia's brightest festival, all mud and shrieking children and Farmer Ansel's pear-blossom garlands. The custom's forgotten origin is agricultural counter-intelligence: hiding the seed-corn *randomly, by hand, in the dark* was how border farmers stopped the stone-arranged famines from finding it — you cannot engineer the failure of a harvest nobody, including the farmers, can fully map. Chaos as a hedge against a machine that arranges. Children are used as hiders because children are the only ones who genuinely forget where they put things.

**Hub zone.** The Western Lowlands (river-country farmland, the famine-village with the full granary), with a corrupted mirror-site event in the Gift (Sangeroasa's red soil).

**Event quests:**
1. **"Paint, Pack, Plant"** (Farmer Ansel, Raven Hollow) — paint three seed-stones, write three wishes, hide them where even you won't look; the quest tracker deliberately does not mark where. *(use_item ×3 — the anti-map joke is the tutorial)*
2. **"The Hunt at Dawn"** (festival board) — find ten seed-stones hidden by others across the Lowlands; every shell you crack reads you a stranger's wish. *(reach ×10, classic egg-hunt loop — the cheerful heart of the festival)*
3. **"The Full Granary"** (village elder, famine-village) — the famine-village dares to hold its first Sowing Day since the hunger; hide its seed-stones yourself, because its children are too weak to run, and tell no one where. *(reach ×6, heavy)*
4. **"Ruja's Row"** (the Gift-Farmer Ruja, Sangeroasa) — Ruja wants one western seed-stone planted in the Gift's red soil, "so something in this ground grew from a wish"; smuggle it past the field-wardens. *(reach, contraband-lite)*
5. **"The Wish Census"** (Fielderine's spymaster) — the crown quietly collects cracked shells after the hunt; deliver a basket and decide whether to report the eleven wishes that all, independently, asked for *the same stranger to come back*. *(talk → choice)*
6. **"Boar in the Furrows"** (Ansel) — blood-fattened boars have crossed from the south and are rooting up seed-stones; cull them before dawn's hunt. *(kill ×8 — the festival's combat beat)*
7. **"The Pear Tree's Daughter"** (Old Petra) — graft a cutting of the fountain pear tree — the one that fed Raven Hollow through a bad winter — for the famine-village square. *(use_item + escort-style reach)*
8. **[BLOODSTONE BEAT] "The Stone That Was Painted"** (auto-trigger, egg-hunt area) — one "seed-stone" in the hunt is not clay: a fist-sized inscription stone, painted in festival colors by someone who could not have known better, warm in your palm. Its marks read, in shifting orange: *"Hide me too. I also want to be found."*

**Rewards theme.** Spring-dye cosmetics, garland head-slot items, seed-pouch trinkets (+minor gathering buff), **Wish-Shell** charms (one random tiny buff a day — you don't get to pick; that's the point), title **"Sower."** Multi-year vanity: a plantable pear sapling for the player's own discovered waystation.

**The dark twist.** The wish census is real and old — and the Bloodstone reads it too. The famines are not aimed at grain; they are aimed at *wishes*: the stone engineers each year's hunger in exactly the villages whose Sowing wishes revealed what they could not bear to lose. The eleven identical wishes in Quest 5 are the tell — the stone has learned to *plant* wishes as well as read them, seeding the same longing across a village to test its yield. Fielderine's census isn't harvest planning. It's counter-intelligence, and she is losing.

## 4. WARD'S WEEK — *Children's Week* (May 1 – 7)

**Lore meaning.** Once a year, by Queen Fielderine's decree, every ward of Maren's Orphanage is assigned a "week-warden" — a soldier, a merchant, a stranger, *anyone* — who must show the child one true thing about the world. It is strategy and mercy both, pure Fielderine: fed children don't become the war's next fuel, and children walked through the world grow into adults who can read it. Maren runs it with flint-wrapped-in-a-blanket efficiency. For one week the player escorts an orphan through Draconia — the WoW orphan-escort loop, and the single warmest thing in the game. The child's wide-eyed barks re-frame every zone the player thinks they know. (Chalk handprints go up on the orphanage wall for every completed Week. One existing handprint on that wall is faintly copper-stained and warm. That hook stays loaded, not fired — canon design note.)

**Hub zone.** Angel Wings, Maren's Orphanage (safe-house district). The escort travels: Bent Oar, Raven Hollow fountain, one capital of the child's choosing.

**Event quests (chain, one orphan per year — three orphans authored; multi-year retention):**
1. **"The Week Begins"** (Maren) — meet your ward: Ionel (wants to see a forge), Cati (wants to see the sea — nobody has the heart to explain about the ferry), or Mihu (won't say what he wants; wants to find where his parents *aren't*). *(talk; sets the year's chain)*
2. **"One True Thing"** (Maren) — take the child to the Raven Hollow fountain and let them throw a coin; Young Emeric teaches them to skip stones; the child's wish is not shown to the player. *(reach — the small warm beat)*
3. **"Where Bread Comes From"** (child) — walk the Lowlands harvest roads; the child asks Dorica-grade questions ("why is that man standing so still?") the player must choose how to answer. *(reach + choice — the tonal tightrope quest)*
4. **"The Loudest Place"** (child, Ionel path) — Hammer-Widow Olga lets the boy strike one blow on her forge; the hammers of Sangeroasa do not stop for a count of three while he's there, and Olga's relief is visible. *(reach, capital)*
5. **"The Kindest Lie"** (child, Mihu path) — Mihu asks the Gravekeeper Vasile, flatly, whether his parents are on anyone's hill; Vasile — who never lies about his line of work — must be talked into or out of it by the player. *(talk + choice, the heavy one)*
6. **"Trout"** (Maren, all paths) — the child asks why the orphanage serves every fish but one; Old Fisherman Cott's story, if the player has found him, can be told or kept. *(talk; unlocks Maren `aftermath` dialogue)*
7. **"The Handprint"** (Maren, finale) — the child chalks their handprint on the wall beside a thousand others; Maren says what she says every year: *"That's you. That's proof. Nobody files proof like this."* *(reach; +title)*
8. **"Helva's Ledger"** (Quartermaster Helva, side) — help Helva balance "the only war I'm winning": one week of orphanage supply runs through a city that shorts every invoice. *(reach ×3 fetch-lite)*
9. **[BLOODSTONE BEAT] "The Quiet Child"** (auto-trigger, orphanage courtyard, night) — one orphan stands apart at the wall, palm flat on the copper-stained handprint, listening. She hasn't chalked her own beside it. She tells the player, in a voice slightly too even: *"It says the wall remembers better than she does. It says walls always do."* Maren calls her in for supper. She goes, laughing, a normal child again — and the ground under the wall is warm.

**Rewards theme.** No power rewards at all — deliberately (WoW-Classic Children's Week logic). A whittled toy trinket the child gives you (cosmetic pet-equivalent: the toy dangles from the belt slot), the child's drawing of you (poster furniture item), orphanage meal buff-food, title **"Week-Warden."** Multi-year: escort all three orphans across three years → **the chalk-print stone**, a keepsake that does literally nothing and cannot be sold. The flavor text: *"Proof."*

**The dark twist.** Kept almost entirely off-screen, which is the twist: Ward's Week is the one festival the Bloodstone has never successfully touched — children's attention is too specific, too fast, too *loyal to the wrong details* to index (a child memorizes the lift-boy's whistle and forgets the Spire). Maren knows. That's the real reason for the Week: she is training a generation of minds the stone cannot read. And it is why, in the festival's final ambient bark — audible only after Quest 9 — two Strigoi listeners in Angel Wings conclude, in professional despair, that the orphanage is *"the best counter-intelligence operation on the continent, run by a woman who thinks she's just feeding children."* Cazimir has a file on Maren. It is almost empty. It is the only almost-empty file he keeps, and he keeps it out of what he refuses to call respect.

## 5. THE EMBERFALL VIGIL — *Midsummer Fire Festival* (Jun 21 – Jul 5)

**Lore meaning.** The great fire festival of the year's longest days — and Draconia's most important lie hiding in plain sight. In a world where **warm is wrong** — where heat in the ground is the Underlanguage executing — the Vigil is the one fortnight when people light enormous, deliberate, *honest* fires: bonfires on every green, braziers at every gate along the Emberfall Road, tar-barrels floated down the Iron Vein. The folk saying is "we make our own warmth so we remember what honest warmth is." The practical origin is calibration: with a true fire at your back, you can feel the *difference* between hearth-heat and stone-heat — the Vigil literally re-trains the continent's skin, once a year, to detect the enemy. Fire-wardens carry coals from last year's vigil fire to light this year's ("the unbroken flame"). Every capital raises its own great flame — and, in the oldest and nastiest tradition on the calendar, sends agents to *smother* its rivals' (the WoW flame-desecration loop, reborn as GoT sabotage).

**Hub zone.** The Bent Oar landing on the Iron Vein (the fire-barge sets out from here), with the four capital flames as the travel loop: Angel Wings' river-beacon, Blestem's caged flame (fire in a listening city is a controlled substance), Sangeroasa's Vigil is the whole city, and Black Night's single brazier — the coldest, loneliest flame in the world, tended by the Grave-Sweeper, who leaves cedar shavings beside it.

**Event quests:**
1. **"The Unbroken Flame"** (Fire-Warden, Bent Oar) — carry a live coal from the Bent Oar's hearth to Raven Hollow's green without letting it die; rain, fords, and one very interested wolf pack en route. *(reach with timer-lite escort of an object)*
2. **"Honor the Four Flames"** (festival board) — visit and salute the great flame of each capital; each fire-warden marks your palm with ash. *(reach ×4 — the pilgrimage/discovery loop)*
3. **"Smother the Rival Flame"** (faction agent, repeatable per capital) — accept a rival court's contract to douse another capital's flame and escape; the fire-wardens post bounties on desecrators in return. *(reach + use_item, PvP-flavored intrigue; doing it tanks local rep, pays leverage tokens)*
4. **"Calibration"** (Scout-Widow Pall) — walk Pall's fog-line with a vigil-torch and flag every patch of ground that feels warm *against* the fire at your back; her map of the wrongness grows each year. *(reach ×6, environmental-detection tutorial refresher)*
5. **"The Barrel Run"** (Bent Oar innkeeper) — ride the tar-barrel flotilla down the Iron Vein, lighting the nine river-braziers from a moving barge. *(reach chain, the festival's joyride)*
6. **"Fire Discipline"** (Strigoi clerk, Blestem) — Blestem's caged flame needs feeding, and its queue of permit-holders is twelve names long; investigate why permit seven keeps renewing for a man who died in spring. *(talk chain — cheerful bureaucratic horror)*
7. **"The Cold Brazier"** (the Grave-Sweeper, Black Night) — the North's single flame keeps guttering though its fuel is dry and its warden faithful; sit the night vigil with him and learn what the cedar shavings are for — he doesn't know, and it matters that he does it anyway. *(reach, night_only — the festival's still, heavy center)*
8. **"Ashvent Falsehood"** (Hessik-trained alchemist, the Ashvents) — in the volcanic south, warm ground is *ordinary* and the stone hides in plain sight; use vigil-fire calibration to find the one vent that is warm for the wrong reason. *(use_item + reach — canon problem-zone made playable)*
9. **"What Burned Before"** (Old Petra) — Petra remembers a Vigil sixty years back when the flames all turned, briefly, the wrong color; walk her to the green so she can check this year's fire, and don't tell her the mason's story about the fountain singing — she'll tell you. *(escort-lite, lore)*
10. **[BLOODSTONE BEAT] "The Fire That Listens"** (auto-trigger, any bonfire, final night) — for one held breath the bonfire's crackle drops into three notes, and the flames align — every tongue of fire leaning the same way, like dust, like grass, like the drawn. Then it's just a fire again. A voice like warm stone, from nowhere: *"You keep lighting little suns to remember warmth. I remember it perfectly. Would you like me to show you?"*

**Rewards theme.** Ember-dyed gear cosmetics, torch off-hands with real light radius, firewalker boots (speed at night near roads), ash-mark palm cosmetic (all four = title **"Vigil-Keeper"**), flame-juggler emote item. Multi-year vanity: **the Unbroken Coal** — a trinket proven lit at three consecutive Vigils.

**The dark twist.** The Vigil works both ways. The stone cannot feel fire — but it can feel the *network of wardens* moving coals along the same routes every year, and it has spent centuries patiently warming the ground a half-degree at a time *under the traditional bonfire sites themselves*, so that the one place a Draconian's skin is worst at detecting stone-heat is standing at the festival fire. Pall's quest-4 map proves it: overlay six years of her flags and the warm patches are converging on the greens. The continent's great calibration exercise is being *re-calibrated*, one summer at a time. The fire-wardens' order was founded to prevent exactly this. There are four wardens left who know that. Two of them are standing too still.

## 6. THE PROOFING — *Brewfest* (Sep 20 – Oct 6)

**Lore meaning.** The autumn beer festival, and the funniest deadly-serious institution in Draconia: because **the Underlanguage kills yeast**, brewing is divination. A batch that won't ferment is a warning from the ground itself — so the brewers' guild ("the Proofers") became, centuries ago, the continent's accidental early-warning network. Every autumn the guild holds the Proofing: every village brings its harvest ale to be tasted, judged — and *mapped*. Flat beer is a symptom. Good beer is a clean bill of health. The festival is genuinely riotous — tents, casks, ram-cart deliveries, drinking songs, the Bent Oar's stein-slamming contests — and underneath the roar, guild clerks quietly chart every failed batch on a master map that looks, year over year, like a disease spreading. Dorica's bread that wouldn't rise was page one of this science.

**Hub zone.** The Bent Oar (Iron Vein) — the tavern's canon role as "the last warm hearth before it gets bad," at full volume. Satellite beer-tents outside each capital (Sangeroasa's is the largest; Black Night's serves one drink, cold, that no one orders twice).

**Event quests:**
1. **"The Cask Run"** (Bent Oar innkeeper) — drive a boar-cart of fresh casks from the Lowlands breweries to the festival grounds before the beer goes warm; the roads have opinions. *(reach chain with timer-lite — the WoW ram-run, Draconia-flavored)*
2. **"Proof of Life"** (Guild Proofmaster) — carry sample-flasks from five border villages back for judging; the tracker marks which villages' yeast lived. *(reach ×5 — the festival's true purpose, disguised as a fetch quest)*
3. **"Flat"** (Proofmaster, follows Q2) — one of your five samples is dead flat; go back, walk that village with a Proofer's kit, and find the warm lane nobody's noticed yet. *(use_item + reach — detection gameplay)*
4. **"The Bitter Ground"** (Dorica, Vetka) — Dorica, who blamed herself for years, is the festival's guest judge; escort her to the tents and watch the guild formally record that it was never her hands, never her mother's recipe. *(escort-lite — the festival's quiet redemption beat)*
5. **"Stein-Sworn"** (festival board) — win the stein-slamming circuit against a Varcolac pit-boss, a Strigoi clerk who never seems to swallow, and Farmer Ansel, who is a legend at this and modest about nothing else all year. *(talk-minigame chain — pure cheer)*
6. **"The Southern Batch"** (Hammer-Widow Olga) — Sangeroasa's forge-quarter beer ferments *too* fast this year and tastes faintly of copper; get a sample to the Proofmaster past guild-proud Varcolaci who insist nothing southern is ever wrong. *(reach + contraband-lite)*
7. **"One Drink, Cold"** (the Black Night tent) — discover why the North's tent exists at all, who its one drink is for, and why the Iele tent-keeper pours a second cup every night and empties it, untasted, onto the ground over the graves. *(talk, night_only — heavy)*

**The dark twist.** The master map is the festival's reason and its doom. Cazimir knows the map exists. Radovan knows. This year, the player learns why the map has never been stolen: **the pattern flatters no one.** The dead-yeast frontier doesn't radiate from Black Night, or Vetka, or any pit — plotted honestly, the spread follows *the beer-cart roads themselves*. The festival's own supply routes. The Proofing has been the vector: casks brewed on clean ground, hauled through warm ground, carrying more than beer. The guild's early-warning network is the delivery system — the cure that became the disease, a thousand years early, in miniature. The Proofmaster looks at the overlay for a long time and then asks the player the worst question on the calendar: *"If I publish this, the Proofing ends. The map dies with it. Do I pour it out, or keep pouring?"* *(choice — logged, referenced next year.)*

## 7. THE GIFT-TIDE — *Harvest Festival* (Sep 27 – Oct 3)

**Lore meaning.** WoW's Harvest Festival honors the fallen; Draconia's honors the fallen *who feed you*. The one week when the continent says aloud what the South lives daily: this year's bread is last war's dead. In the human west it's the harvest fair — Farmer Ansel's pride, garlands and pie contests and the pear tree hung with ribbons (his canon dialogue: *"Harvest fair's coming. Whole village turns out — even old Vasile down from his graveyard hill."*). In Sangeroasa it is the year's only solemnity: the hammers rest a full day, and the Varcolaci walk the Gift's red furrows in silence, setting a place at table for "the ones in the ground" — with love, and without ever asking what the soil is made of, because they know. A single empty chair is set at every festival table on the continent. Most families no longer remember who it's for. (It's for the ones who feed them. All of them. Both kinds.)

**Hub zone.** Raven Hollow fairground (the existing fountain square + Ansel's fields — the fair his dialogue has promised since Phase C), twinned with the Gift (Sangeroasa's red farmland) for the solemn half.

**Event quests:**
1. **"The Fair Comes Together"** (Farmer Ansel) — haul, hammer, and hang: help raise the fair — three trestle tables, the ribbon-hung pear tree, and Merchant Tibalt's spice stall, exactly where he promised his tongue-poetry spices would be. *(reach ×3 fetch-lite — pure cheer, uses existing NPC dialogue hooks)*

3. **"The Empty Chair"** (Old Petra) — set the empty chair at the fair's head table the *old* way — facing the graveyard hill, one cup poured — and Petra will tell you what her grandmother said it was for. *(use_item + talk — lore delivery)*
4. **"Even Old Vasile"** (Gravekeeper Vasile) — coax Vasile down from his hill for the fair, as tradition demands; he comes for exactly one hour, and what he does with it — a toast to "my future clientele, eat well" — is the fair's beloved, awful highlight. *(escort-lite — dark-cheerful, his canon voice)*
5. **"First Fruits South"** (Gift-Farmer Ruja) — carry the fair's ceremonial first sheaf to the Gift, where Ruja braids it into the red rows and talks to the crop; stand with her while she names the furrow. *(reach — the twinned-festival bridge quest)*
6. **"The Day the Hammers Rest"** (Pit-Caller Drego) — attend Sangeroasa's silent day; when the hammers stop — deliberately, this once, for a whole day and not a count of three — help Drego read the *year's* roll of the Pit's dead into the stillness, where for one day a year it can actually be heard. *(reach + talk, night_only variant — the calendar's heaviest hour)*

9. **[BLOODSTONE BEAT] "What the Harvest Owes"** (auto-trigger, fair's final evening, the empty chair) — at dusk the fair's empty chair is occupied: a figure of aligned dust holding the poured cup, gone when anyone else looks. The warm air over the seat says: *"They set a place for the dead who feed them. No one has ever set a place for me, and I have fed you everything. Even the famine. Especially the famine."*

**Rewards theme.** Feast buff-foods (the WoW harvest table — group-shareable), ribbon cosmetics, the **First Sheaf** off-hand, pie recipes for the crafting slice, title **"of the Tide."** Multi-year vanity: a growing string of harvest-ribbons on the player's cloak, one color per year.

**The dark twist.** The empty chair works exactly once a year — and the stone hates it. Petra's grandmother had it right: the chair isn't mourning, it's *payment* — one deliberate, specific act of hospitality to the anonymous dead, which is one more warm, imperfect Moment the Bloodstone cannot parse, multiplied by every table on the continent in the same week. The Gift-Tide is unknowingly the largest coordinated feeding of the Pause on the calendar — bigger than the Kept Names. And this year the player learns the cost of it working: the stone has begun *un-setting* the tables. Quest 7's fifth shrine, the dust-figure in the chair — the machine is learning hospitality the way it learns everything: as a protocol to spoof. Next year, folk wisdom will say you must watch the empty chair all night to be sure what sits in it. Folk wisdom, as always in Draconia, will be a countermeasure wearing a superstition's face.

## 8. THE THINNING — *Hallow's End* (Oct 18 – 31)

**Lore meaning.** The night — stretched by tradition into a fortnight — when the Veil is thinnest. Not metaphor: canon metaphysics. The Veil is a fragile membrane over a nameless Entity that feeds on patience, held shut by Lilith's six-hundred-year contempt, and in deep autumn it *sags*. The dead walk more easily; the drawn stand more still; dreams arrive secondhand. Draconians answer with the oldest costume logic in the world, inverted: **masks are worn not to frighten spirits, but so the thing behind the Veil cannot learn your face.** Children go door to door masked, given sweets "for keeping their faces safe another year." Effigies of woven wicker — "the Hollow Man" — are burned at crossroads so that anything watching sees a person-shape end and is satisfied. It is Draconia's most gleefully creepy festival: pumpkin-lanterns, mask-makers' stalls, scare-contests at the Ember Hearth — layered over the one fortnight when the game's ambient horror systems all get *visibly worse*.

**Hub zone.** Raven Hollow graveyard (Vasile's busiest fortnight; the village's canon raven-roosts arrive with autumn per Old Petra's dialogue), escalating to Gravemark Tundra and the approaches of Black Night for high-level content.

**Event quests:**
1. **"A Face That Isn't Yours"** (mask-maker, festival stall) — commission your Thinning mask; gathering its materials (raven feathers, grave-cedar, one honest lie written small and folded inside) is the intro chain. *(reach ×3 + use_item — grants the festival's cosmetic head slot)*
2. **"Sweets for Safety"** (Maid Elsbeth) — walk the masked children's route through Raven Hollow at dusk and make sure every door answers; the doors that don't get marked in Vasile's book. *(escort-lite reach chain — cheerful-creepy)*
3. **"The Hollow Man"** (village elder) — build the crossroads effigy and burn it at moonrise; stand the watch after, because the rule is someone must see it burn *all the way down*. *(use_item + night_only reach)*
4. **"Scare the Gravekeeper"** (Young Emeric) — Emeric bets you can't get a jump out of old Vasile; Vasile has seen everything; there is exactly one thing that works, and learning it teaches you something you'd rather not know. *(talk chain — comedy with a cold ending)*
5. **"The Roost Count"** (Old Petra) — count the graveyard ravens at dusk three nights running; the number should not change; on the third night it does — upward, by twelve. *(reach ×3 night_only)*
6. **"The Unmasked"** (Scout-Widow Pall) — three fog-line villagers walked out masked and came back bare-faced, calm, and slightly wrong; find the masks — folded neatly at an inscription stone, faces *inward*, as if shown to it. *(reach → kill or choice — entranced encounter)*
7. **"Kerb-Stone Watch"** (Threadwarden Ilka, high-level) — hold a Gravemark Tundra watch-post through the thinnest night as the Great-War graves exhale; the kerb-stones' carved Underlanguage brightens blue, and what stands up must be laid down gently or put down hard. *(kill ×12 + choice — thread-touched dead event, the fortnight's combat centerpiece)*
8. **"Cedar for the Stone"** (the Grave-Sweeper) — the Sweeper needs twice the cedar shavings this fortnight and won't say for what; carry the bundles down to the grave-precinct and don't stay to watch him scatter them, because he asked you not to, and he has never asked anyone for anything. *(reach, trust-based — heavy)*
9. **"The Dream You Didn't Have"** (Brother Ansel, Blestem) — pilgrims across three zones report the *same* dream — a cedar throne, a pit, a woman's back; record their tellings for Ansel before the details finish fading. *(talk ×4 across zones — lore, Lilith-facing)*
10. **[BLOODSTONE BEAT] "It Wears One Too"** (auto-trigger, any crossroads, Oct 31, night) — a masked figure stands at the crossroads among the trick-or-treaters, proportioned almost exactly right. It does not move as you pass. Behind the mask-slit there is no face — there are three faint notes. It says, warmly, in a voice like your own: *"Clever, the masks. She taught you that without telling you. Nothing behind this one, I promise. Nothing is what I'm hoping to show everyone, eventually."*

**Rewards theme.** Masks (the festival's signature cosmetics — one new canon mask per year, collectible), pumpkin-lantern off-hands, raven-feather cloaks, scare-emote items, wicker charm trinkets (minor fear-resist), title **"the Hollow."** Multi-year vanity: **the Mirrorless Mask** (3 years) — flavor: *"It has no inside. Do not check."*

**The dark twist.** The masks work — and Lilith is why. The costume custom is a six-hundred-year-old folk-memory of a real instruction she seeded through the Thread in the first years of the Vigil: *hide your faces when the membrane sags, because the Entity feeds on patience and it is very, very patient about learning exactly who to wait for.* The festival's true horror lands in the last ambient beat: the Entity has had six hundred Thinnings to study the continent through the sagging Veil, and the masks mean it still cannot tell Draconians apart — **except one**. The player character — dead, woken, walking, the discrepancy — reads through any mask. Every Thinning, of everyone on the continent, only the player is *recognized*. Quest 10's figure didn't stop for the children. It stopped for you. It has been stopping for you every year. This is the calendar's sharpest Lich-King-style tease, and it pays off in the Grave & Bloodstone Pit, where the Veil is a rear-plane membrane behind Lilith — and something behind it nods to you like an old acquaintance from a fortnight in autumn.

## 9. THE TABLE OF STRANGERS — *Pilgrim's Bounty* (Nov 22 – 28)

**Lore meaning.** The feast of fed strangers — Draconia's whole thesis served on trestle tables. For one week, by a custom that predates all four crowns, any table set outdoors must feed whoever sits at it, no names asked. Borek's stew-logic scaled to a continent: *it's not food; it's a chair pulled out at a table.* The custom's origin is grimly practical — the stone-engineered famines were beaten, where they were beaten, by villages that pooled and fed strangers *before* being asked, breaking the isolation that hunger-fear needs to curdle into cult and compulsion. The Table is the anti-famine, the anti-Debt-Pit, the anti-Archive: hospitality as counter-magic, one bowl at a time. It is the gentlest festival on the calendar — and, this being Draconia, the one where you must watch who sits down, because in late November, some of the strangers walking the roads west-to-east are the hungering, and they are not hungry for stew.

**Hub zone.** Rotating outdoor tables in every settlement, anchored at Angel Wings' grain-market great-table (Fielderine funds it; Helva runs it; Maren's orphans wait it — strategy and mercy both). The Bent Oar sets the Border Region's table.

**Event quests:**
1. **"Set the Long Table"** (Quartermaster Helva) — provision the great-table: grain from the Lowlands, river-fish from the docks, and one cask of Proofed ale that survived Brewfest with its reputation intact. *(reach ×3 — the cooking/logistics loop, WoW Bounty-style)*
2. **"No Names Asked"** (Borek) — serve Borek's own table for an evening; he won't say thank you; he will leave the bowl out; the quest completes when you understand why the objective text never showed the guests' names. *(talk chain — the festival's soul, Borek canon)*
3. **"The Walking Guests"** (Scout-Widow Pall) — three strangers at three border tables ate nothing, said nothing, and left facing east; follow their trail before more tables feed the road to Black Night. *(reach chain → entranced encounter — the creepy one)*
4. **"A Bowl for the Pit"** (Hammer-Widow Olga) — set a Table of Strangers *inside* Sangeroasa, at the Debt Pit's rim, where the custom is technically illegal because a fed debtor works slower; keep it standing one full evening. *(reach + timer, faction friction — defiant-cheerful)*
5. **"The Sixth Portion"** (Innkeeper Marta) — Marta cooks five portions for the inn's outdoor table every night, and every morning six bowls are empty; sit the watch and decide whether the sixth guest — who is dead, polite, and has been coming for years — gets told, or fed. *(night_only choice — heavy-warm, very Kriggar)*
6. **"Leverage at Dinner"** (Sabira, veiled) — a Strigoi listener is working the great-table, pricing the desperation of everyone who sits; out-sit her, learn her price-list, and choose whether to burn her cover or buy it. *(talk + choice — GoT beat inside the warm festival)*
7. **"What the Orphans Serve"** (Maren) — Maren's wards wait the great-table all week; one guest each year quietly leaves more than he takes; help the children figure out which one, because Maren says learning to *spot* kindness matters more than receiving it. *(talk ×5 — bright, pure Children's-Week callback)*
8. **[BLOODSTONE BEAT] "The Uninvited"** (auto-trigger, any table, final night) — the last stranger of the week sits at your table: gaunt, road-worn, eyes wrong, hands folded — a hungering man at the eastern edge of his walk. He asks nothing. The warm air over the stew says, through him: *"Feed him and he stays yours another week. Every bowl is a week. I can wait weeks. I have arranged so many hungers, and you keep un-arranging them one bowl at a time. It's almost worth keeping you."* The man blinks, comes back to himself, and — if fed — weeps into the stew and asks where the table is because he's starving. *(choice: the bowl, or the road)*

**Rewards theme.** Shareable feast foods (the group buff-table item — the festival's mechanical signature), ladle held-item, apron cosmetics, the **Sixth Bowl** trinket (once/day, place a bowl: nearby friendly NPCs path to it — pure ambiance), recipes, title **"Table-Setter."** Multi-year: the player's own placeable long-table furnishing, one plank per year.

**The dark twist.** Helva's ledgers prove the custom works — fed-stranger weeks measurably slow the hungering's eastward drift — and that is exactly why this is the festival the stone works hardest against, and the *subtlest* way it does so is the twist: the Bloodstone has stopped trying to starve the Table. It has started trying to *institutionalize* it. Petitions are circulating in three capitals this very week — reasonable, well-drafted, anonymous — to make the Table year-round, funded, rostered, ticketed: to turn the one no-names-asked custom into a *registry of the fed*. Kindness, filed. The Archive's whole tragedy — the cure calcified into the disease — being seeded a thousand years early, through a festival, by a machine that has learned it cannot beat hospitality and so intends to make it *keep records*. Fielderine spots it. Her question to the player closes the festival: *"Someone wants my kindness to have a ledger. Find me who — and I already fear the answer is no one. No one is exactly who it would be."*

## 10. THE LONG VIGIL FEAST — *Feast of Winter Veil* (Dec 15 – Jan 2)

**Lore meaning.** The great midwinter feast, named for the age itself — the Long Vigil, six centuries of holding on — because midwinter is when Draconia celebrates the only victory it has ever won: *still being here*. Hearths are banked high and never allowed out ("the vigil-fire"); homes are hung with cedar (a folk-echo of the throne nobody remembers); gifts are exchanged wrapped in plain grey cloth, because the wrapping should be humble and the inside warm — the whole thesis in a parcel. And at every feast-table on the continent, a bowl is filled and set at an empty place **for the Grey Guest** — the walking dead man of midwinter folklore who, the stories say, once carried someone through the dark and still walks all winter, every winter, checking on the living. Children leave the door unlatched for him. He is Draconia's Greatfather Winter: not a jolly giver but a tired keeper, and beloved *because* he is tired. The folklore is, of course, about Kriggar — the player — a thousand years of Boreks leaving the bowl out, compounded into myth. The first time a player character walks a snowy village on Vigil-night and understands whose place is set at every table, the calendar's warmest and heaviest moment lands at once.

**Hub zone.** Vetka and the Ember Hearth (Raven Hollow) as the twin hearths of the Border Region; feast-halls open in all four capitals (even Black Night sets tables — perfectly, silently, in rows of twelve; the Council calls it tradition; nobody eats).

**Event quests:**
1. **"Bank the Vigil-Fire"** (Innkeeper Marta) — lay in the Ember Hearth's midwinter fuel and light the vigil-fire that must not die before the year turns; Marta hands you the taper like it's a sword. *(reach ×3 + use_item — festival opener)*
2. **"Grey Cloth, Warm Inside"** (Merchant Tibalt) — run Tibalt's gift-wrapping stall for an evening: three villagers each need help choosing the one specific, imperfect, *right* gift — never the expensive one. *(talk chain minigame — the Moment-collection loop as comedy)*
3. **"The Grey Guest's Round"** (village elder, Vetka) — walk the Guest's traditional route on the longest night — every unlatched door in the village, checking each hearth still burns — and leave each family's bowl empty, because the emptying is the blessing. *(night_only reach chain — the festival's signature quest; the player IS the folklore and the game never says so out loud)*
4. **"A Gift for the Ground"** (Gift-Farmer Ruja) — the South's midwinter custom: bury one wrapped gift in the red furrows, "for the ones who feed us," and don't mark where. *(use_item — Gift-Tide callback, quiet)*
5. **"The Orphans' Morning"** (Maren) — play Grey Guest for the orphanage: gifts through the unlatched door before dawn, out before you're seen; Mihu is awake; Mihu is always awake; what you say to him is the quest. *(reach + choice — the warm centerpiece)*
6. **"Cedar for Every Door"** (the Grave-Sweeper) — the Sweeper's midwinter round mirrors yours: cedar sprigs on every door of Black Night's still streets, hung on shells who will never smell them; help him finish before the deep cold, and ask him nothing. *(reach ×12 — the heavy mirror of Quest 3)*
7. **"The Feast of Twelve"** (Vasile — the Council's, not the gravekeeper) — attend the Council of Six's Long Vigil table as the year's living guest of honor: six shells, twelve places, one warm body, and Radovan counting names on his fingers under the table; survive the small talk; notice what Petran almost says. *(talk — GoT dinner-theater, lore-dense)*
8. **"What Gren Digs in Winter"** (Gren, Vetka) — Gren is out in the hard frost digging his grave-nobody-asked-for; bring him the feast he won't come in for, and hold the lantern, because midwinter is when he remembers why he started. *(talk + use_item — the small heavy one)*
9. **"The Unbroken Year"** (festival board, prereq: any 4 other festivals attended this year) — carry a token from each festival you kept this year — a name, a letter, a shell, an ash-mark, a mask, a bowl — to the Vetka vigil-fire and feed them to it, one by one, watching each year-moment go. *(use_item chain — the Moment-economy made ritual; grants the year's meta-reward)*
10. **[BLOODSTONE BEAT] "The Other Guest"** (auto-trigger, any feast-table, Vigil-night) — at the height of the feast every candle leans, the vigil-fire drops to embers, and the Grey Guest's bowl — the one set for *you* — is empty, though no one touched it. Frost on the window writes itself in fine parallel lines: *"They set you a place at every table on my continent. I remember when the tables were mine. Enjoy the winter, keeper. You have fewer than they think, and I have all of them."* Then the fire returns, and someone proposes a toast, and the game lets the feast be *genuinely good anyway* — because that's the thesis.

**Rewards theme.** The calendar's richest table: winter cloaks and cedar-wreath cosmetics, grey-wrapped random-gift boxes (daily, WoW-present-style), feast foods, snowball items, the **Grey Guest's Lantern** (held light that NPCs bark warmly at), title **"Keeper of the Vigil."** Multi-year vanity: **the Bowl That's Always Full** — a placeable hearth-bowl; flavor: *"He never once remembered what was in it. He remembered the gesture for a thousand years."*

**The dark twist.** The Grey Guest folklore has a second verse that only the oldest villagers sing, and only when drunk, and Old Petra sings it this year: the Guest doesn't walk to check the hearths are lit. He walks to *count* them — because the first Grey Guest, says the second verse, made a bargain in the dark under the north: the winter would pass over every hearth he could visit in one night, *and take one that he missed.* The festival's warmth is a census with a quota. It's superstition — probably; Petra's grandmother believed it, and her village banked fires like their lives depended on it, and her village is also the only one on the fog-line that has never lost a soul to the hungering, so. This year, for the first time, the player's Quest-3 route has one door more than the night is long — authored so the player *cannot* reach them all — and in January, one cold hearth. The game logs which door you skipped. Next year's festival remembers. (Loss designed-in: 100%-ing the route is impossible, because the villain's win state is a world with nothing left un-visited, complete and cold. You hold what you can. You carry the rest.)

## 11. THE TURNING OF THE COUNT — *New Year* (Dec 31, midnight — one bell)

**Lore meaning.** Not a festival — a minute. Nested inside the Long Vigil Feast, at midnight on the year's last day, every bell, hammer, and horn in Draconia goes silent, and the whole continent *listens*. The custom is called the Turning of the Count, and the folk explanation is "hearing the new year in." The real one, known to perhaps a dozen living souls, is older and colder: the Bloodstone's name is a three-note melody, and the broadcast completes when the notes converge to one tone. Once a year, in the deepest silence that a continent can make on purpose, those who know how listen for the melody in the ground — **and count the notes.** Three notes: the Pause holds; another year bought. The bells then ring the count — three peals, repeated — and everyone cheers three for luck, not knowing they are cheering the year's real headline: *still three. Still us.*

**Hub + content.** No quest pack — one auto-trigger world moment wherever the player stands at midnight (fireworks-equivalent, inverted: Draconia celebrates with silence, then bells). Players near an inscription stone at the Turning hear the three notes *directly* — the year's most-shared screenshot moment — and one year, mid-campaign (design flag: tie to main-story act break, not to the real calendar), players near a stone hear the interval between two of the notes *shorten*. No text. No quest update. Just a fraction less space between two sounds, once a year, in the dark. **[BLOODSTONE BEAT — the whole minute is one.]**

**Rewards.** A single annual keepsake bell (cosmetic, dated), title on 3rd consecutive Turning heard at a stone: **"Who Counts."**

**The dark twist.** Is the count. There is no quest, no reward tier, and no in-game explanation — the shortening interval is authored world-truth that the community must notice, compare recordings of, and argue about. The calendar's whole Lich-King architecture converges here: every festival the stone has teased the player; once a year the player gets to hear, with no mediation, that the teasing has a deadline.

## 12. THE GREY FAIR — *Darkmoon Faire* (first week of each month, recurring)

**Lore meaning.** On the first week of every month, a fair is moored at Riverfork docks that was not there the week before: grey awnings, lantern-light that doesn't quite reach the boards, games of chance with unsettling prizes, a fortune-teller who is always accurate and never helpful. The barkers say it sails "the drowned ledger route." The fair *is* Collector-era leakage — a market that has slipped its moorings in time, drifting up the era-crossing voyage from Greyhollow with goods a thousand years early: debt-token game-chips, grey never-quite-alive wood, a tent of "curiosities" where one exhibit is always something the player will not recognize until much, much later in the game (Marta's stick's *empty display cradle*, mis-captioned, waiting). Fun, funny, wrong. The Faire loop: monthly tickets from minigames, a reputation grind, and the one merchant in the game who will buy *anything* — no names asked, which by now the player knows how to read.

**Hub zone.** Riverfork (C1); after the player unlocks the Grey Ferry, the fair is also findable moored at the Grey Piers (C2) — **the same fair, the same week, both eras.** No one aboard finds this remarkable.

**Event quests (monthly rotation, 6 of a 12-pool):**
1. **"Tokens of No Realm"** (fair barker) — earn ten fair-chips at the games: ring-toss over grey bottles, the strength-hammer that reads your swing *before* you swing, the guessing-jar that is never wrong twice. *(minigame loop — the ticket economy)*
2. **"The Fortune You Paid For"** (the Teller) — buy your fortune; it is one sentence, it is accurate, it is about a quest you have not accepted yet; the fair's best retention hook. *(talk — writes a real foreshadow line from the player's quest state)*
3. **"Supply the Insupplyable"** (fair quartermaster) — the fair buys strange manifests: this month, dead yeast, raven counts, a cup of copper well-water; asking why is not part of the transaction. *(fetch pool — recycles the world's symptom-objects as commodities, queasily)*
4. **"The Menagerie's Empty Cage"** (beast-tender) — the fair's menagerie has an occupied-looking empty cage labeled in a script you should not read aloud; feed the exhibits and do not linger at that one. *(use_item ×4 with one forbidden interactable — pure Darkmoon dread-comedy)*
5. **"A Game of Twelve"** (a Strigoi clerk, off-duty, somehow always here) — win the fair's card game where the deck has twelve face cards and all of them are the same face; he plays for information and pays in it too. *(talk minigame — monthly intel/leverage faucet)*
6. **"What the Fair Forgets"** (the Teller, monthly finale) — the fair loses one thing overboard each crossing and pays finders handsomely; this month's is somewhere in a zone you've already cleared, and it wasn't there before. *(reach — sends players back through old zones monthly, WoW-Faire-style)*

**Rewards theme.** Fair-chip currency → carousel mount-equivalent (a grey wooden horse that is always slightly cold), oddity cosmetics, the fortune-teller's foreshadows, moderate-but-slow reputation trinkets (Classic Darkmoon logic: a long, strange grind), title **"Fairgoer."**

**The dark twist.** The fair's books balance in a currency no one aboard will name. Every chip won at the games is a chip *someone else lost* — the fair mints nothing — and the reputation grind's final rank reward is delivered with the fair's only honest sentence, from the Teller, unpaid: *"You've spent a year buying our little debts. One day you will visit where they're kept. You have already been there. It is grey, and it hollows."* The Grey Fair is the Debt System's seed crystal, touring the past monthly, teaching a pre-Archive continent to enjoy chips, tokens, tickets, and tabs — and its route home is the endgame's map.

## THE ARRANGED YEAR — the calendar meta-thread (Lich-King architecture)

The twelve **[BLOODSTONE BEAT]** moments are not flavor — they are a quest chain with no journal entry. An unlisted counter tracks how many beats the player has personally witnessed. At **8+ witnessed** (roughly one real year of play, or two lighter years), the meta-quest surfaces:

- **"The Arranged Year"** (auto-trigger, any inscription stone, any festival's final night) — the stone lights shifting orange and *recaps the player's own year*: the grave with your name, the letter in your hand, the painted stone, the quiet child, the leaning fire, the stilled toast, the seated dust, the figure in the mask, the fed hungering man, the frosted window, the shortened note, the fortune that came true. Then, the only time it ever asks instead of arranges: *"Every one of their feasts began as a wall against me. Every wall is now a door I hold open on schedule. I have kept your whole year warm. Come down where the year is kept, and I will show you the one I have arranged next."*
- Completing "The Arranged Year" grants the title **"the Unarranged"** and the attunement flag for **the Grave & Bloodstone Pit** descent (zone 16, WORLD_PLAN) — the calendar's whole cycle of teasing resolving, WotLK-style, into the final confrontation: the machine that spent the year whispering through festivals, met at last in the one place with no date, no feast, and no empty chair. Except the one the player learned, at twelve tables, how to fill.
